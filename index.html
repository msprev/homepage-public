I am Senior Lecturer in Philosophy at the School of Philosophy, Psychology and Language Sciences, University of Edinburgh.

My primary research interests are in philosophy of mind, philosophy of science, metaphysics, and philosophy of language. I am interested in how these issues in these areas intersect with current cognitive science. I am also interested in how cognitive science integrates with the wider humanities.

Research areas:

  1. The embodied, extended, and distributed turn in cognitive science

  2. Using computation to explain cognition

  3. The role of sub-personal representations in cognitive science

Seeing Minds Online: A new project that uses the Turing Test to probe the psychology of online chat.

Mind Network: I am one of the founders and organisers of the Mind Network.
