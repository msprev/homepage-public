Strategy for writing a first class essay

25 April 2017
 pdf

A first class essay shouldn’t…

  1. Be a magnum opus
  2. Describe every possible view in the field
  3. Include all your thoughts or reading for the course

A first class essay needn’t…

  1. Be your last word on the topic
  2. Conclusively establish your claim—be honest about this!
  3. Solve a major problem (e.g. the mind-body problem, the problem of induction, external world scepticism, etc.)

A first class essay should…

The claim

  1. Have a claim
  2. Right size of claim
  3. Claim is clearly defined and non-trivial
  4. Clear statement of claim in your own words
  5. Use a simple example to illustrate your claim
  6. Justify carefully why your claim is important

The argument

  1. Break argument up into individual steps
  2. Slow down and argue each step carefully
  3. Be explicit about the justification for each step and how the steps fit together
  4. Put yourself in shoes of a sceptical reader
  5. One good reason for accepting a claim is better than many half-baked ones

The exposition of literature

  1. Demonstrate understanding of the material of the course
  2. Explain the work of others in your own words; avoid unexplained jargon
  3. Explain with simple worked examples
  4. Go beyond the lectures/core reading
  5. Cite work of others appropriately1

The essay writing

  1. Avoid spelling and grammatical errors2
  2. Write well3
  3. Good, accurate introduction and conclusion
  4. Structure your essay — syntax should mirror semantics
  5. Cut all irrelevant material
  6. Quotations may only attribute a specific view to a specific author; they do not explain or argue on your behalf
  7. Read what you write carefully and redraft to ensure what you say cannot be mistaken
  8. Make clear your original contribution
